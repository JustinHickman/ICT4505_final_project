Who we are

Sigma Phi Epsilon (SigEp) TX Kappa Chapter was founded on November 1, 1971, at the University of Texas at Arlington. Since then, SigEp has grown to a 40 man chapter and owns the largest fraternity house on campus. Last semester, SigEp achieved an overall GPA of 3.4 (the highest across all fraternities). Nationally, around 15,000 SigEp undergraduates are on 228 college campuses each year. They are among the more than 321,000 lifetime brothers who have joined since 1901.

Mission Statement

SigEp is redefining fraternity on college campuses across the country. We complement a man’s education by delivering a premier student experience in one of the most formative times of his life. Through SigEp, men strengthen their character, build leadership and interpersonal skills, and learn to develop healthy, lifelong relationships that are essential to a successful and fulfilling life.

SigEp was founded with the Cardinal Principles of Virtue, Diligence and Brotherly Love. Our mission is “Building Balanced Men,” which is achieved through a commitment to Sound Mind and Sound Body. We are committed to working with our host institutions as a valued partner in higher education.

Our Program

SigEp revolutionized the fraternity movement in 1991 by unveiling the Balanced Man Program (BMP), a concept of single-tiered membership and continuous development that begins the day a brother joins. SigEp believes the stereotypical fraternity experience does not fulfill the needs of today’s college male. The BMP offers an experience focused on scholarship, leadership, professional development and life skills. Members learn to live their best lives through unique, rewarding programming tailored to fit their needs and prepare them for the journey of life ahead. Learn More
